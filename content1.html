FREVO

FREVO

Frevo is a Brazilian artistic expression comprising music and dance, performed mainly during the Carnival of Recife. Its quick frenetic and vigorous rhythm draws upon the fusion of musical genres such as marching music, Brazilian tango, square dance, polka and pieces of classical repertoire, performed by martial bands and fanfares.

    BAIÃO

    BAIÃO

    The baião refers to a type of Northeastern Brazilian music style, dance routines, and cuisine. Many artists who played baião songs also sang forrobodó (forró) styles. At its core, the rhythm of baião is all about the zabumba’s beat. The zabumba is a bass drum played with both hands at the same time.

  • Luiz Gonzaga was a Brazilian musician, songwriter, poet, and one of the most influential figures of Brazilian popular music in the twentieth century. He has been credited with presenting the rich universe of Northeastern musical genres to Brazil, having popularized the musical genre baião. According to Caetano Veloso (a very popular MPB and Bossa Nova artist), he was Brazil’s first significant cultural event with mass appeal.     MARACATU

    MARACATU

    Maracatu is a type of secular Afro-Brazilian music. In this genre, a folk song is called a toada. The lyrics are usually a mixture of Portuguese and Yoruba languages. The toadas with dancing, chants, and poetry mark the Maracatu Nação’s carnival culture. The main instruments used in Maracatu are the wooden alfaia drum, tarol snare drums, gonguê bells, and the African shekeres.

    FORRÓ

    FORRÓ

    The term forró refers to a musical genre, a rhythm, dance, and the event where forró music is played and danced. Forró is an integral part of the culture of the Northeastern Region of Brazil. It encompasses various dance types and several different musical genres. The genres of forró include pé de Serra, universitário, eletrônico and many others. The shortened word forró can refer to an event, music, or dance routine. Forró festivals are now held in many places worldwide, such as Russia, the UK, and the US. Luiz Gonzaga is highly regarded as the father of forrobodó.
