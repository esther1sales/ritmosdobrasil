SERTANEJO

Sertanejo originated in the rural area of central Brazil - primarily in the states of São Paulo, Paraná, Minas Gerais, Goias, Mato Grosso and Mato Grosso do Sul. It consists of romantic ballads, and/or stories about rural life and struggles - but it is also popular dance music.

MARÍLIA MENDONÇA

  • 07/22/1995 - 11/05/2021
  • Marília Mendonça - Ao Vivo
  • Patroas 35%
  • Marília was a Brazilian singer, songwriter and instrumentalist, posthumously recognized in Brazil as the Queen of Sofrência, a subgenre of sertanejo music, and has been recognized for her contribution to female empowerment by revolutionizing the universe of sertanejo music. Unfortunately, she was in an airplane crash and passed away in 2021.

    • MAIARA & MARAISA

  • 03/2004 - Present
  • No Dia do Seu Casamento
  • Ao Vivo em Portugal
  • Maiara & Maraisa is a sertanejo musical duo consisting of twin sisters Maiara Carla Henrique Pereira and Carla Maraísa Henrique Pereira. Both of them are singers, songwriters, multi-instrumentalists and businesswomen. They are also known as icons of the subgenre "feminejo", sertanejo music that is focused on the woman's point of view. They were friends with Marília Mendonça and were the last ones to collaborate with her before the accident.

    • JORGE & MATEUS

  • 26/05/2005 - Present
  • Ao Vivo em Goiânia (2007)
  • É Simples Assim (2022)
  • It was through a mutual friend in 2005 that Jorge and Mateus had the chance to sing together. The chemistry was so strong that they began performing as a duo. Their first show was on May 26, 2005, at a nightclub in Itumbiara (a city in Goiás). The duo then recorded a CD in Mateus's garage, featuring some of their compositions and new versions of classic sertanejo songs. In 2007, their first official work, the CD/DVD “Ao Vivo Em Goiânia,” was released by Universal Music.
