TECHNOBREGA

TECHNOBREGA

Tecno brega is also called techno tacky. It’s a form of electro-music that originated from Belém in northern Brazil. The genre’s tracks are mostly remixes of old songs released in the 80s. This redistribution of music was highlighted in the documentary, Good Copy Bad Copy. So far, tecno brega is still underground and it’s not highly represented in concerts or music festivals.

FELIPE CORDEIRO

  • 1984 - Present
  • Kitsch Pop Cult
  • Transpyra
  • Brazilian musician Felipe Cordeiro is from Belem, Brazil. His style of music falls within the broad category of Latin music, with an emphasis on styles like carimbo, guitarrada, and brega. Cordeiro is renowned for his distinctive style, which combines modern electronic beats with ancient Amazonian rhythms to produce music that is both nostalgic and infectious.

    • GABY AMARANTOS

  • 08/01/1978 - Present
  • Treme
  • Purakê
  • Born into a family of samba enthusiasts, Amarantos was influenced at an early age by the Caribbean radio frequencies that reached equatorial Belém, along with brega, lambada, Clara Nunes, Kraftwerk, and Juan Luis Guerra. Later, the electronic aparelhagem soundsystem parties in Jurunas would have a strong impact on her musical direction. She started the Tecnobrega movement with her band Tecno Show in 2002.

    • PABLLO VITTAR

  • 11/01/1993 - Present
  • Vai Passar Mal
  • Noitada
  • Know off-stage as Phabullo Rodrigues da Silva, this singer was the first drag queen to be have a stable musical career in Brazil, influencing many others. Pabllo is mainly a pop singer, although her music incorporates other genres. Her debut studio album Vai Passar Mal mixes pop with elements of electronic music and Brazilian genres such as tecno brega, arrocha, funk carioca, and forró.
